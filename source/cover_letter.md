# Draft ISR Cover Letter: Private AI, Public Answer Work, and Certification at Stack Overflow

**Alternative target:** *Information Systems Research*  
**Date:** April 5, 2026

Dear Editors,

Please consider our manuscript, *Private AI, Public Answer Work, and Certification at Stack Overflow*, for publication in *Information Systems Research*.

The manuscript studies how private AI changes public answer work once it becomes a plausible outside option for routine troubleshooting. Using the official Stack Overflow `2025Q4` dump, we build a linked archival sample of `2,035,885` single-focal-tag questions and `2,391,883` matched answers across `16` technical domains from `2020-01-01` through `2025-12-31`.

The paper's central claim is narrow. Private AI can leave a platform visibly active while weakening the link between early response and later public certification. In more AI-substitutable domains, the public queue becomes more residualized, incumbent answer capacity thins, recent entrants become more visible in early and endorsed answer roles, early visible answers convert into accepted certification less often, and larger first-versus-accepted role gaps are associated with slower endorsed resolution and weaker downstream certification.

The paper is disciplined about identification. We do not claim complete direct observation of AI use in Stack Overflow, and we do not claim a clean one-date treatment effect. We instead add targeted evidence that narrows the key critiques: a same-setting disclosed-AI corpus, one strict question-side AI-ban timing layer around `2022-12-05`, JetBrains developer-survey microdata that calibrate the substitution premise across the focal tags, and two external direct-AI validation settings (`DevGPT` for bounded question-like color and `AIDev` for certification-focused public review).

We believe the manuscript fits *ISR* because it studies a platform-level reallocation process at the intersection of generative AI, online knowledge communities, and digital governance. Adjacent work emphasizes contribution volume, answer characteristics, or knowledge seeking. This paper instead studies how private AI reshapes visible public labor, and why that matters for downstream certification in a reusable public archive.

Timing diagnostics remain mixed, so the paper avoids a sharp-shock narrative. The bridge is presented as a downstream consequence layer, not mediation. These boundaries are explicit in the manuscript.

The manuscript is not under review elsewhere.
